# KFC Product Detail Page Redesign

## Overview

As KFC’s digital menu expanded, the product detail page became a bottleneck in the ordering flow. Small usability issues compounded at scale, slowing decision-making and creating uncertainty right before checkout. This work focused on simplifying that moment without disrupting familiar patterns or existing systems.

**Role:** Product Designer  
**Client:** KFC (via Photon)  
**Timeline:** 6 months  
**Platforms:** Desktop, Mobile Web, App

---

## TL;DR

- 88 percent task success across platforms
- 20 percent reduction in average task completion time
- Improved clarity and confidence in a high-volume customization flow

## The Challenge

New meal options and customization made the product detail page harder to use when customers needed clarity. Feedback highlighted three issues that slowed decision-making.

## The Problem

As new meal options, sides, and customization rules were added over time, the product detail page became harder to use at the exact moment customers needed clarity.

Customer behavior and feedback consistently pointed to three issues that slowed decision-making and introduced friction in a critical conversion moment.

### 1. Overwhelming Layout on Mobile

The product image and content took too much space, forcing users to scroll excessively on smaller screens.

### 2. Fragmented Customization Flow

Customization options for sauces were repeated, allowing only one selection. This made the flow longer and confusing.

### 3. Unclear Feedback Before Checkout

Users had limited visibility of their selections, making it easy to lose track and feel uncertain before adding items.

---

## Design Principles

Rather than redesigning the page wholesale, I anchored the work around a small set of principles that guided every decision.

- **Reduce decision friction** by minimizing unnecessary steps
- **Surface key choices earlier** in the flow, especially on mobile
- **Group related options** to make customization easier to scan
- **Preserve familiar patterns** to avoid relearning in a high-traffic experience

---

## The Solution

Instead of introducing new features, the solution focused on a targeted set of changes designed to remove friction while maintaining consistency with existing patterns.

### 1. A More Balanced Mobile Layout

The layout improved by reducing image dominance and highlighting details.

_Addresses: Overwhelming Layout on Mobile_

---

### 2. Simplified Customization in One Place

Customization options are now clearer, enabling users to select without repeating steps.

_Addresses: Fragmented Customization Flow_

---

### 3. Clearer Feedback While Ordering

A stronger visual hierarchy improved feedback, making it easier for users to review selections before checkout.

_Addresses: Unclear Feedback Before Checkout_

---

## Impact

- 88 percent task success rate across all platforms
- 20 percent reduction in average task completion time
- 90 percent of participants reported higher satisfaction
