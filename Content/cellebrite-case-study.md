# Cellebrite Website Redesign

## Overview

Redesigning a complex B2B SaaS marketing site to improve content clarity, reduce bounce, and better communicate product value to enterprise audiences.

Cellebrite’s website had grown dense and difficult to navigate, making it hard for users to understand offerings or find relevant information. This work focused on clarifying content hierarchy and improving wayfinding without disrupting existing business messaging.

**Role:** UX Designer  
**Client:** Cellebrite  
**Timeline:** 4 months  
**Platforms:** Desktop, Mobile Web

---

## TL;DR

- 25%+ increase in page views
- 60%+ reduction in bounce rate
- Clearer product messaging and improved navigation

I led the UX redesign, working closely with stakeholders to simplify structure and improve how complex information was presented.

## My Role

I owned the UX design for the Cellebrite website redesign, from problem framing through final delivery. I collaborated with stakeholders, marketing, and engineering to align business goals with user needs and create a scalable foundation for future growth.

---

## The Problem

As Cellebrite’s product portfolio expanded, the website became harder to navigate and understand. User behavior and stakeholder feedback highlighted several issues impacting engagement.

### 1. Overloaded Content Hierarchy

Key information was buried under dense layouts, making it difficult for users to quickly grasp product value.

### 2. Poor Wayfinding

Users struggled to understand where they were on the site or how to navigate between related content.

### 3. Lack of Consistency Across Pages

Inconsistent layouts and patterns increased cognitive load and reduced trust.

---

## Design Principles

The redesign was guided by principles focused on clarity, scalability, and ease of navigation.

### Clarify Before Adding

The goal was not to add content, but to simplify and reorganize what already existed.

### Guide Users Through Complexity

Navigation and hierarchy were designed to help users move confidently through complex information without feeling lost.

### Design for Scale

Layouts and components needed to support future growth without requiring repeated redesigns.

### Create Visual Consistency

Standardized patterns and a shared design system helped reduce cognitive load and improve trust.

---

## The Solution

The solution focused on simplifying structure and improving clarity across the site.

### 1. Restructured Content Hierarchy

Content was reorganized to surface key information earlier and reduce visual clutter.

_Addresses: Overloaded Content Hierarchy_

---

### 2. Improved Navigation and Wayfinding

Navigation patterns were clarified to help users understand where they were and how to explore related content.

_Addresses: Poor Wayfinding_

---

### 3. Scalable Design System

A flexible design system was introduced to ensure consistency and support future updates.

_Addresses: Lack of Consistency_

---

## Impact

- 25%+ increase in page views
- 60%+ reduction in bounce rate
- Improved engagement across key pages

The redesign improved how enterprise users consumed complex information while giving internal teams a scalable foundation for future growth.
