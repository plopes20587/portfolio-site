# SeneGence E-commerce Redesign

## Overview

Redesigning a direct-to-consumer e-commerce experience to simplify navigation, reduce checkout friction, and support both customers and distributors shopping on the same platform.

SeneGence’s existing site had grown organically over time, resulting in inconsistent navigation, mismatched mobile and desktop experiences, and a checkout flow that created unnecessary barriers. This work focused on modernizing the shopping experience while preserving the needs of a multi-audience commerce model.

**Role:** Product Designer  
**Client:** SeneGence  
**Timeline:** 6 months  
**Platforms:** Desktop, Mobile Web

---

- Over $7M in revenue generated post-launch

## My Role

I owned the UX design for the SeneGence e-commerce redesign from problem definition through final delivery. I partnered closely with stakeholders, engineering, and business teams to restructure navigation, improve product discovery, and reduce friction in checkout across desktop and mobile.

---

## The Problem

As the platform evolved to support both customers and distributors, the shopping experience became increasingly difficult to navigate. Behavioral data and stakeholder feedback pointed to several issues that hurt confidence and conversion.

### 1. Confusing Navigation and Information Hierarchy

Users struggled to find key product information quickly, often bouncing between pages or abandoning sessions altogether.

### 2. Inconsistent Desktop and Mobile Experiences

Mobile and desktop layouts did not align, creating confusion for users switching devices and reducing trust in the experience.

### 3. High Friction During Checkout

Mandatory account creation and unclear steps added unnecessary friction at the point of purchase.

---

## Design Principles

Rather than redesigning for aesthetics alone, the work was guided by principles focused on clarity, confidence, and conversion.

### Prioritize Product Discovery

Navigation and hierarchy needed to help users quickly understand what products were available and how to compare them without feeling overwhelmed.

### Reduce Commitment Barriers

Checkout flows were designed to remove unnecessary blockers, allowing users to complete purchases with minimal friction.

### Design for Multiple Audiences

The experience needed to support both distributors and customers without forcing either group through confusing or irrelevant paths.

### Maintain Brand Consistency

Visual and interaction patterns were standardized to create a cohesive experience across pages and devices.

---

## The Solution

The redesign focused on simplifying structure and reducing friction across the shopping journey.

### 1. Streamlined Navigation and IA

Navigation was reorganized to surface key categories and product information earlier, improving findability and reducing bounce.

_Addresses: Confusing Navigation_

---

### 2. Unified Responsive Experience

Layouts and interactions were aligned across desktop and mobile, creating consistency and improving user confidence.

_Addresses: Inconsistent Desktop and Mobile Experiences_

---

### 3. Simplified Checkout Flow

Account creation requirements were relaxed and checkout steps clarified, making it easier for users to complete purchases.

_Addresses: High Friction During Checkout_

---

## Impact

- 80K+ conversions within six months of launch
- Over $7M in revenue generated post-redesign
- Meaningful lift driven by improved navigation and reduced checkout friction

The redesign demonstrated how simplifying structure and removing barriers can directly impact conversion in a multi-audience e-commerce environment.
