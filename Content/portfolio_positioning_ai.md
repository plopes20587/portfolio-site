# Portfolio Positioning & Messaging (AI‑Aware)

This document defines the language, tone, and positioning used across my portfolio, resume, and online presence. It is meant to keep messaging consistent while subtly signaling AI fluency without overclaiming.

---

## Core Positioning

I’m a senior product designer focused on shopping experiences and customer journeys across B2C and B2B products. I solve messy, real‑world problems by simplifying complex systems, designing end‑to‑end flows, and balancing user needs with business constraints.

I use modern tools — including AI — to think, explore, and ship faster, while still owning the judgment, craft, and final decisions.

---

## How AI Fits Into My Work

AI is not the product — it’s part of my process.

I use AI to:
- Explore multiple ideas quickly in early discovery
- Pressure‑test assumptions and edge cases
- Speed up repetitive or exploratory work
- Focus more time on decision‑making, systems thinking, and craft

I do **not** position myself as an AI engineer or ML specialist. I’m a product designer who understands how to work effectively alongside intelligent systems.

## Resume Language (Approved Usage)

Safe, accurate phrases that hint at AI capability:

- “Modern design workflows”
- “AI‑assisted ideation and iteration”
- “Rapid prototyping under ambiguity”
- “Using modern tools to accelerate exploration and delivery”

Example resume bullet:
> Use AI‑assisted tools to explore solutions quickly, iterate on flows, and accelerate delivery under tight timelines.

---

## Case Study Framing (AI‑Aware)

AI should appear in **process**, not outcomes.

Good places to reference AI:
- Exploration phase
- Working under tight timelines
- Future considerations

Example:
> To move quickly, I used AI to explore alternate flows and stress‑test assumptions before refining final designs.

Avoid claiming AI‑powered features unless they truly exist.

---

## Interview Talking Point

If asked how I use AI:

> I use AI as a thinking and acceleration tool — especially early on — to explore ideas, challenge assumptions, and reduce time spent on repetitive work. It helps me move faster, but the design decisions and responsibility always stay with me.

- No buzzwords
- No overclaiming
- Focus on judgment, speed, and problem‑solving
